# Sound Symbolism Reference — Lexicon Branding

Based on David Placek's work with linguists Will Leben and Philip Cohen at Lexicon Branding. Sound symbolism is the study of how the human brain associates certain sounds with meanings, emotions, and attributes — independent of the word's actual definition.

---

## Consonant Properties

### Power / Reliability
- **B** — One of the most reliable sounds in English. Grounded, solid, dependable. (BlackBerry, Boeing, BMW)
- **D** — Direct, decisive, grounded. (Dell, Dyson, Dasani)
- **M** — Warm, maternal, comforting. (Microsoft, Mercedes, Mastercard)

### Speed / Innovation
- **K/C (hard)** — Sharp, crisp, cutting-edge. (Kodak, Kindle, Kayak)
- **Z** — Fast, innovative, electric. (Zara, Zoom, Azure)
- **X** — Extreme, experimental, futuristic. (Xerox, Xbox, SpaceX)
- **P** — Precise, crisp, clean. (Pentium, PayPal, Pinterest)

### Daring / Energy
- **V** — Daring, velocity, vital. (Viagra, Corvette, Vercel, Vivid)
- **T** — Technical, tight, controlled. (Tesla, Twitter, TikTok)

### Softness / Approachability
- **S** — Smooth, flowing, sophisticated. (Sonos, Samsung, Swiffer)
- **L** — Light, lyrical, elegant. (Lexus, LinkedIn, Lululemon)
- **F** — Flowing, fresh, friendly. (Febreze, Facebook, Firefox)

## Vowel Properties

- **A (short)** — Active, alert (Amazon, Apple, Asana)
- **A (long/open)** — Expansive, aspirational (Azure, Acura)
- **E (long)** — Easy, breezy, energetic (Etsy, Everlane)
- **I (short)** — Quick, nimble (Instagram, Intel)
- **I (long/ee)** — Bright, light, uplifting (Dasani final syllable, Fiji)
- **O (long)** — Bold, powerful, authoritative (Sonos, Roku, Volvo)
- **U (long/oo)** — Cool, smooth, futuristic (Hulu, Lulu, Ubuntu)

## Structural Patterns

### CVCV (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Vowel)
The most naturally accessible pattern. How children learn language: mama, dada, papa. Instantly processable.
- Sonos, Roku, Hulu, Navan, Vivo

### CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant)
Punchy, memorable, one-syllable impact.
- Slack, Zoom, Box, Vim

### CVCCV / CVCVC
Two-syllable with more complexity — still accessible.
- Vercel, Figma, Notion, Linear

### Three+ syllables
Work when each syllable contributes meaning or sound energy.
- Febreze (feb-REEZE), Pentium (PEN-tee-um), Accenture (ak-SEN-chur)

## Combination Principles

1. **Contrast creates energy**: Pair a soft vowel with a hard consonant (So-NOS: soft S + bold N + strong S)
2. **Rhyme and rhythm**: Internal repetition aids memory (TikTok, Fitbit, Snapchat)
3. **The landing matters**: End on a strong consonant for authority (Slack), soft vowel for warmth (Tesla)
4. **Two syllables is the sweet spot**: One is too abrupt for a company name (Box), three+ needs each syllable earning its place
5. **Global check**: Avoid combinations that sound negative in major languages. Lexicon uses 97 linguists in 57 countries for this.

## The "Say It 10 Times" Test

Say the name out loud 10 times quickly. If it:
- Gets easier each time → **good** (processing fluency working)
- Gets harder or feels awkward → **bad** (tongue-tripping = memory-tripping)
- Makes you smile or react → **great** (emotional resonance = tension zone energy)
