# Watercolor + Paper Cutout Hybrid

A children's book illustration style that combines two distinct rendering techniques in the same image. Organic, structural elements are rendered in soft watercolor/colored pencil. Accent elements are rendered as flat, matte paper cutouts that look physically collaged onto the page. Inspired by Christie Matheson's "Tap the Magic Tree."

## When to Use

- Content about education, growth, learning, childhood
- When you want warmth and whimsy without being childish
- Topics where you need both organic depth and graphic clarity
- When you want to draw the eye to specific elements (the cutout pieces pop)
- Articles about choice, mixing, assembling from parts

## The Two Layers

This style requires deciding IN ADVANCE which elements get which treatment:

**Watercolor layer** (organic, structural, background):
- Tree trunks, branches, terrain, buildings, furniture, architectural elements
- Skin, hair, clothing on people/characters
- Ground, sky, water, shadows
- Anything that needs depth, volume, or natural texture
- Rendered with visible brushwork, color gradation, soft edges, paper grain showing through

**Paper cutout layer** (graphic, accent, foreground pop):
- Leaves, flowers, petals, stars, hearts
- Small birds, butterflies, bees
- Geometric shapes used as labels or icons
- Key objects you want to POP visually (a book, a backpack, a musical note)
- Rendered with clean flat edges, solid matte colors, no gradients, slight visible paper texture
- Should look like they were physically cut from colored construction paper and glued down

## Color Application

- **Watercolor elements**: Soft, muted tones — warm browns, gentle greens, dusty blues, blush pinks. Colors blend and pool naturally. Visible brushstrokes.
- **Cutout elements**: Bolder, more saturated solid colors — leaf green, burnt orange, golden yellow, cherry red, sky blue. Each cutout piece is ONE solid color. No blending within a single cutout.
- **Background**: Clean white or very light warm off-white. Lots of breathing room. The white space is part of the composition.
- **Overall palette**: Limited to 5-7 colors total. The contrast between soft watercolor and bold cutouts creates visual interest without needing many hues.

## Technical Direction

```
STYLE: Children's book illustration combining two techniques. Structural elements (trunk, branches, ground, buildings, people) are painted in soft watercolor with colored pencil texture — visible brushwork, natural color gradation, paper grain showing through. Accent elements (leaves, flowers, small objects, icons) are flat paper cutouts — clean edges, solid matte colors, no gradients, as if physically cut from construction paper and collaged onto the watercolor painting.

RENDERING: The watercolor layer comes first — soft, organic, textured. The cutout elements sit ON TOP, looking slightly lifted from the surface. There should be a visible contrast between the two techniques: one is painterly and soft, the other is graphic and crisp.

TEXTURE: Watercolor areas show paper grain, brushstrokes, natural pigment pooling. Cutout areas show slight paper fiber texture but are otherwise flat and clean-edged. The juxtaposition of these textures is the defining characteristic.
```

## Example Prompt Structure

```
Create an illustration in a children's book style that combines watercolor painting with paper cutout collage.

CONCEPT: [Scene description, specifying which elements are watercolor and which are cutout]

STYLE: Structural and organic elements (specify: trunk, ground, building, etc.) are rendered in soft watercolor with colored pencil texture — visible brushwork, gentle color gradation, paper grain. Accent elements (specify: leaves, flowers, icons, etc.) are flat paper cutouts — clean edges, solid matte colors, no gradients, looking like construction paper pieces collaged onto the painting.

COMPOSITION: [Framing, focal point, generous white space]

COLORS: Watercolor palette uses soft muted tones — warm brown, dusty green, blush pink. Cutout pieces use bolder saturated solids — leaf green, golden yellow, burnt orange, cherry red. Limited to 5-7 total colors.

TEXTURE: Watercolor shows brushstrokes and pigment pooling. Cutouts are flat and matte with clean edges. White background with breathing room.

AVOID: Digital gradients, photorealism, busy compositions, too many colors, text, dark or moody tones, overly detailed backgrounds.

FORMAT: [16:9 / 1:1 / 9:16]
```

## Deciding What Gets Cutout Treatment

The rule of thumb: **cutout the things that represent CHOICE or VARIETY.** In an article about personalized learning, the different learning modalities (a book, a laptop, a trumpet, a seedling) would be cutouts — they're the mix-and-match pieces. The backpack or desk or child would be watercolor — they're the stable, grounding structure.

This maps well to the article's metaphor: the SYSTEM is organic and natural (watercolor), the CHOICES families make are distinct, visible, and portable (cutouts).

## Good Subjects for This Style

- Educational content (learning paths, curriculum choices)
- Seasonal / nature themes (trees, gardens, growth)
- Anything about assembling or mixing components
- Family and childhood scenes
- Content about growth, branching, possibility
