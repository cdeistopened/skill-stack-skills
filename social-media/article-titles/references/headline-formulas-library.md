## Formula 1: How-to

### Structure
"How to [Achieve Desired Outcome] in [Timeframe/Steps]"

### Variations
- "How to [Result] in [Time]"
- "How to [Solve Problem] with [Method]"
- "How to [Goal] without [Objection]"
- "How to [Result] even if [Common Obstacle]"

### Examples

**Educational content:**
- "How to Write a Bestseller in 30 Days"
- "How to Build a 6-Figure Online Business from Scratch"
- "How to Master Public Speaking in 10 Simple Steps"

**OpenEd applications:**
- "How to Start a Microschool in 6 Months (No Teaching Credential Required)"
- "How to Create a Custom Learning Plan That Actually Works"
- "How to Help Your Kid Love Learning Again in 90 Days"

### Element Combinations
Works best with: **Problem + Process** or **Goal + Process**

### When to Use
- Content explains a clear method or process
- You have step-by-step instructions
- Timeline creates urgency
- Audience wants actionable guidance

## Combining Multiple Formulas

The most powerful headlines often combine elements from 2-3 formulas:

**Example 1:** How-to + Without + Timeframe
- "How to Start a Microschool in 90 Days Without a Teaching Credential"
- Combines: Formula 1 + Formula 7 + specific timeline

**Example 2:** Authority + List + Benefit
- "Tim Ferriss's 7 Habits That Doubled My Productivity"
- Combines: Formula 6 + Formula 2 + clear outcome

**Example 3:** Question + Number + Negativity
- "Are You Making These 5 Homeschool Mistakes That Hurt Learning?"
- Combines: Formula 3 + Formula 11 + problem focus

**Example 4:** Time-Saving + List + Target
- "I've Tested 20 Curricula. Here Are the 5 Best for Unschoolers"
- Combines: Formula 15 + Formula 2 + Formula 12

## Testing and Iteration

**Generate variations using:**
1. Pick 3-5 formulas that match your content type
2. Create 2 variations per formula (10-15 total)
3. Apply 10 Commandments to optimize
4. Test using 4 U's and Anatomy criteria
5. Track which formulas perform best for your content/audience

**Common patterns:**
- B2B/Professional: Formulas 6, 12, 14, 15 perform well
- Consumer/Parent: Formulas 1, 2, 7, 9 perform well
- Thread hooks: Formulas 2, 4, 6, 13 perform well
- Newsletter subject lines: Formulas 1, 3, 4, 7 perform well
