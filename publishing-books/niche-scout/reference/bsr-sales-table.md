# BSR-to-Sales Reference Table

Approximate daily sales by Best Seller Rank. Based on Dale Roberts' estimates and cross-referenced with Sean Dollwet's benchmarks from 175 KDP episodes.

## Kindle eBooks

| BSR Range | Approx Sales/Day | Signal |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| 1–1,000 | 25+ | Bestseller territory — fist fight to compete |
| 1,000–5,000 | 12–25 | Very competitive — established authors dominate |
| 5,000–10,000 | 5–12 | Competitive but possible for strong entries |
| 10,000–30,000 | 1–5 | **Sweet spot** — proven demand, beatable competition |
| 30,000–100,000 | <1 | Minimum viable — book is selling but slowly |
| 100,000+ | ~0 | Dead or near-dead — no meaningful sales |

> "I want to see a rank of anywhere between 10,000 and 100,000. That's about roughly 1 to 12 book sales per day." — Sean Dollwet

## Dollwet's BSR Benchmarks (Top 20 Books in a Niche)

| What to Check | Target | Why |
|---------------|--------|-----|
| All top 20 books | Under 30,000 BSR | Confirms demand is real |
| Books under 5,000 BSR | No more than 5 | Keeps competition manageable |
| Most books | 10,000–30,000 BSR | The ideal zone for new publishers |
| At least 7 of 10 | Under 100,000 BSR | Minimum viable demand |
| Self-published books | At least 2–3 on page 1 | Proves indie can compete |

## Platform-Specific BSR Targets

| Platform | Good BSR | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Kindle eBooks | Under 50,000 | Most competitive; most publishers start here |
| Paperback | Under 100,000 | Higher royalties; reference/cookbooks do better |
| Audiobook (Audible) | Under 100 search results | Least competitive; under-served in most niches |

> "Some topics do well on one platform and not the other. Just because Kindle is not profitable doesn't mean that keyword is bad." — Sean Dollwet

## Revenue Math

At **$4.99 Kindle price** (70% royalty = ~$3.49/sale):

| Sales/Day | Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | ~$105 | ~$1,270 |
| 3 | ~$314 | ~$3,810 |
| 5 | ~$524 | ~$6,350 |
| 10 | ~$1,047 | ~$12,700 |

The business model works through **catalog accumulation** — most first books sell 1–3 copies/day. A 10-book series at 2 sales/day each = 20 sales/day = ~$2,100/month.
