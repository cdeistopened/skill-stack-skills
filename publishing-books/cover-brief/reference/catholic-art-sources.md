# Catholic & Religious Public Domain Art Sources

Quick reference for finding high-quality public domain art for Catholic book covers.

## Best Museum Collections (Open Access, High-Res)

### Metropolitan Museum of Art (New York)
- **URL:** metmuseum.org → search, filter by "Open Access"
- **License:** CC0 — no restrictions
- **Strengths:** Medieval manuscripts, Renaissance altarpieces, Baroque devotional, Byzantine icons
- **Search terms:** "saint", "madonna", "monastery", "prayer", "contemplation", "mystic", "monk"

### National Gallery of Art (Washington DC)
- **URL:** nga.gov → search the collection
- **License:** Open Access, free download
- **Strengths:** Italian Renaissance, Northern Renaissance, American religious art
- **Note:** Click "Download" on individual works — high-res TIFF available

### Art Institute of Chicago
- **URL:** artic.edu → search the collection, filter CC0
- **License:** CC0 on qualifying works
- **Strengths:** Medieval, Impressionist, strong decorative arts for texture/pattern backgrounds

### Rijksmuseum (Amsterdam)
- **URL:** rijksmuseum.nl → Rijksstudio
- **License:** CC0
- **Strengths:** Dutch Golden Age, Rembrandt's biblical scenes, Vermeer's interiors (contemplative mood)

### Wikimedia Commons
- **URL:** commons.wikimedia.org
- **License:** Varies — check each image. Look for "PD-old-100" or "PD-Art" tags
- **Strengths:** Largest collection, covers every period and artist
- **Weakness:** Quality varies wildly. Always find the highest-res version

### Library of Congress
- **URL:** loc.gov → Prints & Photographs
- **License:** Generally no known restrictions
- **Strengths:** Historical photographs, engravings, manuscripts, maps

## Best Artists by Catholic Book Genre

### Mystical Theology / Contemplation
- **Fra Angelico** (1395-1455) — luminous, devotional, serene. Perfect for prayer/mysticism books.
- **Zurbarán** (1598-1664) — monks in white robes against dark backgrounds. Dramatic, contemplative.
- **Georges de La Tour** (1593-1652) — candlelight scenes, meditation, quiet devotion.
- **Philippe de Champaigne** (1602-1674) — Jansenist austerity, powerful portraits of religious figures.
- **El Greco** (1541-1614) — mystical intensity, elongated figures, spiritual ecstasy.

### Saints & Hagiography
- **Caravaggio** (1571-1610) — dramatic lighting, raw emotion, powerful saint scenes.
- **Ribera** (1591-1652) — intense saint portraits, penitent figures.
- **Murillo** (1617-1682) — gentle, accessible, popular Marian and saint imagery.
- **Giotto** (1267-1337) — foundational, iconic, immediately recognizable saint cycles.

### Monastic Life / Benedictine
- **Zurbarán** — definitive painter of monastic life (Carthusians, Franciscans, Mercedarians)
- **Chardin** (1699-1779) — quiet domestic scenes that evoke monastic simplicity
- **Medieval manuscript illuminations** — search "benedictine manuscript" or "monastic illumination" on Wikimedia

### Scripture / Biblical
- **Rembrandt** (1606-1669) — deeply human biblical scenes, extraordinary light
- **Gustave Doré** (1832-1883) — iconic Bible illustrations, high contrast engravings
- **James Tissot** (1836-1902) — watercolor Bible series, historically researched, beautiful

### Church History / Apologetics
- **Raphael** (1483-1520) — School of Athens, papal portraits, doctrinal scenes
- **Medieval mappae mundi** — symbolic world maps, great for cosmological themes
- **Cathedral architecture photography** (pre-1928 photos are PD)

## Search Strategy for Specific Books

When searching for art for a specific book:

1. **Extract 3-5 key themes** from the manuscript
2. **Identify any saints/figures** referenced by name
3. **Note the historical period** — match era of art to era of text when possible
4. **Search each museum** with theme keywords
5. **Cross-reference Wikimedia** for works by specific artists
6. **Check Google Arts & Culture** for discovery, then download from museum site
7. **Evaluate crop potential** — portraits crop better than landscapes for 5x8 covers

## Rights Verification Checklist

Before using any image:

- [ ] Artist died 70+ years ago (or work published pre-1928)
- [ ] Image is not a modern photograph of a 3D sculpture (photographer may have copyright)
- [ ] Image is not from a museum that restricts commercial use in their ToS
- [ ] High-res version available (minimum 300 DPI at cover dimensions)
- [ ] No modern watermarks or overlays
- [ ] If using Wikimedia: license tag says PD-old, PD-Art, or CC0
