# Amazon KDP Keyword Rules

Official guidelines and restrictions for the 7 backend keyword slots.

---

## The Basics

- You get **7 keyword boxes** when publishing on KDP
- Each box allows up to **50 characters**
- Keywords are **not visible** to shoppers (backend only)
- Amazon uses them to index your book for search

---

## What to Include

### DO use:
- Phrases readers actually search for
- Synonyms and related terms (but not excessive)
- Specific sub-genres or niches
- Setting or time period (for fiction)
- Themes and topics
- Comparable author styles (e.g., "fans of [genre]")
- Reader outcomes (what they'll learn/feel)

### Format tips:
- Use **lowercase** (capitalization doesn't matter)
- **No commas needed** within a box—spaces separate terms
- Combine related terms in one box to maximize coverage
- Order words logically (how a reader would search)

---

## What NOT to Include

Amazon prohibits certain keywords. Violating these can result in book suppression or account issues.

### Prohibited:

| Category | Examples | Why Prohibited |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Your title/subtitle words** | If title is "The Fasting Guide," don't use "fasting guide" | Already indexed; wastes slot |
| **Competitor names** | "better than [Author Name]" | Trademark/misleading |
| **Competitor book titles** | "[Competitor Book] alternative" | Trademark/misleading |
| **Bestseller claims** | "bestseller," "#1 book," "award-winning" | Unverifiable claims |
| **Subjective quality claims** | "best," "amazing," "top-rated" | Unverifiable |
| **Temporary status** | "new release," "on sale," "just published" | Becomes outdated |
| **Common misspellings** | "fasing" for "fasting" | Amazon handles automatically |
| **Quotation marks** | "fasting guide" | Unnecessary, may cause issues |
| **ASIN/ISBN references** | B001234567 | Not a searchable term |
| **Profane/offensive terms** | — | Policy violation |
| **Misleading terms** | Keywords unrelated to content | Deceptive practice |

---

## The Duplication Rule (Critical)

**Do NOT repeat words that appear in your title or subtitle.**

Amazon already indexes your title. Duplicating those words:
- Wastes valuable keyword space
- Does NOT improve ranking
- May actually hurt by signaling "thin" metadata

**Example:**
- Title: "The Benedict Challenge: A 40-Day Lenten Fasting Guide"
- **Avoid:** "benedict," "challenge," "40 day," "lenten," "fasting," "guide"
- **Use instead:** "catholic spiritual discipline," "christian intermittent," "one meal a day"

---

## Character Limits

| Element | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Each keyword box | 50 characters |
| Total boxes | 7 |
| Total available | 350 characters |

**Maximize your 350 characters.** Empty space is wasted opportunity.

---

## Phrase vs. Single Word

**Always prefer phrases over single words.**

| Approach | Example | Effectiveness |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Single word | "fasting" | Too broad, high competition |
| Two words | "christian fasting" | Better targeting |
| Long-tail phrase | "christian fasting guide for lent" | Best—specific intent match |

Long-tail phrases:
- Match specific reader searches
- Face less competition
- Convert better (reader found exactly what they wanted)

---

## Category-Cementing Keywords

Some keywords can trigger automatic category placement by Amazon. This is separate from the 3 categories you select manually.

**How it works:**
- Amazon scans your keywords for category signals
- If keywords match a category's "trigger words," Amazon may add you to that category
- This can expand your reach beyond your 3 selected categories

**Example trigger words:**
- "cookbook" → Cookbooks category
- "devotional" → Devotionals category
- "journal" → Journals category
- "prayer" → Prayer category

**Warning:** This can also remove you from categories if Amazon decides your keywords don't match. Use intentionally.

---

## Ebook vs. Paperback Keywords

Keywords are indexed **separately** for ebook and paperback editions.

- A keyword that boosts your ebook may not affect paperback ranking
- You can (and should) optimize keywords for each format
- Consider format-specific terms: "kindle unlimited" for ebook, "gift book" for hardcover

---

## Updates and Changes

Keywords can be updated anytime through your KDP dashboard:
1. Go to your Bookshelf
2. Click "Edit eBook Details" (or print)
3. Scroll to Keywords section
4. Update and Save

**Recommendation:** Refresh keywords every 3-6 months based on:
- Sales performance
- New search trends
- Seasonal relevance
- Competitor movements

---

## Quick Checklist

Before submitting keywords:

- [ ] All 7 slots filled
- [ ] Each slot ≤ 50 characters
- [ ] No title/subtitle words repeated
- [ ] No competitor names or book titles
- [ ] No bestseller/quality claims
- [ ] No temporary terms
- [ ] Phrases, not single words
- [ ] Logically ordered (as a reader would search)
